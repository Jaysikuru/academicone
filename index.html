About Me

Pushing the Boundaries of Quantum Physics

I'm a Quantum Physics Professor at Stanford University with over 15 years of experience in theoretical and experimental quantum mechanics. My research focuses on quantum entanglement, quantum computing applications, and theoretical foundations of quantum mechanics.

As both a researcher and educator, I strive to bridge the gap between complex theoretical concepts and practical applications. My teaching philosophy emphasizes hands-on experimentation, critical thinking, and interdisciplinary approaches.

I believe in making complex quantum physics concepts accessible through a combination of clear theoretical foundations and hands-on practical applications. My teaching approach emphasizes:

  • Interactive Learning: Using simulations and virtual labs to demonstrate quantum principles
  • Real-world Applications: Connecting theoretical concepts to practical uses in technology and research
  • Interdisciplinary Approach: Exploring connections between quantum physics and other fields
  • Inclusive Education: Creating content accessible to diverse learning backgrounds and styles
